A briefing note

A read on where AI actually fits in UBS's stack, and where it does not

Four facts define UBS's 2026 technology posture. A new Chief AI Office under Daniele Magazzeni. Three hundred forty live AI use cases. The final Credit Suisse IT decommissioning. An Americas wealth franchise named publicly by Sergio Ermotti as the firm's near-term priority. The throughline across all four is the same question. Which layer does each problem belong on.

The thesis

UBS enters the second half of 2026 with 340 live AI use cases, a new Chief AI Office under Daniele Magazzeni effective January 1, the final Credit Suisse IT decommissioning behind it, and a US Americas wealth franchise that Sergio Ermotti has publicly named as the firm's near-term technology and organizational priority.

A portfolio of 340 use cases will not be governed by adding more use cases. It will be governed by the decisions not taken. Which experiments never should have used a large language model. Which workflows were already solved by a database query. Which require the rigor of a rule set and an audit trail rather than a probabilistic response.

That distinction is the work of the Chief AI Office in its first year. It is also the work Eduba does for enterprise clients under a framework called computational orchestration.

The 60/30/10 frame

Our operating view. Firms that skip this distinction pay for LLM inference where Postgres would do the job faster, cheaper, and with better guarantees for a regulated institution.

60% Traditional code Database work, APIs, deterministic pipelines. Solved by engineering, not inference.
30% Rule-based logic Audit trails, compliance, defensible outputs. Solved by rules, not probability.
10% Genuine AI Where language, pattern recognition, and synthesis are the right tool. The keep-and-scale list.

Applied to UBS, the framework resolves the 340-use-case portfolio into three lists. Keep and scale. Consolidate and automate without AI. Retire. The deliverable is not a slide deck. It is a decision record a governance body can defend.
